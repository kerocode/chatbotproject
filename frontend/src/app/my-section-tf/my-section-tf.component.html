I used tensorflow 2.0 to train CNN model to recognize difference between three particular types of Lymphoma.

FL

Follicular lymphoma (FL) is typically a slow-growing or indolent form of non- Hodgkin lymphoma (NHL) that arises from B-lymphocytes, making it a B-cell lymphoma. This lymphoma subtype accounts for 20 to 30 percent of all NHL cases.

CLL

Chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) is cancer that affect lymphocytes. When most of the cancer cells are located in the bloodstream and the bone marrow, the disease is referred to as CLL.

MCL

Mantle Cell lymphoma is typically an aggressive, rare, form of non-Hodgkin lymphoma (NHL) that arises from cells originating in the “mantle zone.” MCL accounts for roughly six percent of all NHL cases in the United States.

as you can see, it is very hard to identify the difference between this three type of cancers, or at least to normal person who have not been studied these type before; moreover, not all doctor can easly know which one is which.
